About Trainer

International Yoga Instructor Samresh Keshyap , an exceptional individual hailing from the historic city of Vaishali, Bihar. His journey into the world of yoga began with early childhood, under the guidance of his grandfather. His academic yoga journey started in 2003 at Dev Sanskriti University in Shantikunj, Haridwar, where he deepened his understanding of this ancient practice.
In 2007, his path led him beyond the borders of India, embarking on a remarkable journey that took him to diverse corners of the globe, including Macau, Singapore, Shanghai, and Dubai. During his extensive travels, he had the privilege of conducting yoga retreats in 15 different countries, transcending cultural boundaries and sharing the profound benefits of yoga.
For him, yoga is not just a profession; it's a calling. He has an unwavering passion for teaching yoga and views it as a means to contribute to the well-being and happiness of society. His unique experiences and his belief in the transformative power of yoga go beyond the physical exercise â€“ it's a way to nurture physical, mental, and spiritual health, ultimately contributing to a harmonious world.
Among his remarkable achievements, he had the honor of teaching yoga to notable figures like Mr. Rajnath Singh, the Defence Minister of India, as well as several renowned actors, actresses, and other famous personalities. His dedication to yoga led to him winning three gold medals in the Uttarakhand Yoga Championships and silver and gold medals in the All India Inter-University Championships.
In his yoga classes, he is known for his motivating words, such as 'do more, gain more,' 'no pain, no gain, try again,' and his emphasis on the importance of connecting with oneself through breath. For him, yoga is more than just physical postures; it's a way to connect with people, to help them improve, and to foster the development of good human beings.
His journey is a testament to the power of yoga in transforming lives and making a positive impact on both individuals and society as a whole. "
